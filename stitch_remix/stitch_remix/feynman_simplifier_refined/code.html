Action Potential

Simplified Explanation

The Core Idea

An action potential is essentially the way nerve cells (neurons) communicate. It's a brief electrical signal that travels down the neuron, carrying information to other cells.

compare_arrows

The Analogy: The Stadium Wave

Think of a neuron like a long row of fans in a sports stadium. When the game is quiet, everyone is sitting down. This is the Resting Potential.

  • Depolarization (Standing up): Suddenly, someone starts a wave. A few people stand up, prompting the people next to them to stand up. In the cell, this is sodium (Na+) channels opening, letting positive charge rush in.
  • Repolarization (Sitting down): As soon as people stand up, they immediately sit back down so the wave can continue past them. In the cell, potassium (K+) channels open, letting positive charge rush out, resetting the balance.
  • Refractory Period (Taking a breath): After sitting down, a person needs a second to catch their breath before they can do the wave again. The neuron also needs a brief moment to reset its ions before firing another signal.
key
Key NEET Takeaway

Remember the sequence: Resting (-70mV) -> Depolarization (Na+ in) -> Repolarization (K+ out) -> Hyperpolarization -> Resting. It's an all-or-nothing event.
